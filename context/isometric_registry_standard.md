# Isometric Registry Standard (v1.7 / v1.8 / v2.0)

Isometric is the voluntary carbon market (VCM) registry Charm Industrial uses to earn carbon credits for CDR projects.

**Version history:** Standard history begins October 4, 2023.
- **v1.7** — used in Charm's Apr-May 2025 verification (350Solutions, July 2025)
- **v1.8** — version referenced in Charm's PDD v1.10 (Sept 2025)
- **v2.0** — current registry version as of early 2026

## Key v2.0 Changes vs. v1.8
- Crediting periods: **10 years** standard; 15 years for DAC and Biogenic CCS (was 5 years max under v1.8 for non-biosphere)
- VVB rotation: max 5 consecutive years; max 5 of any 7 consecutive years
- Initial crediting period start: no earlier than 5 years before registry submission
- No ex-ante credits
- Materiality threshold: 5% (unchanged)

**Note:** Charm's active crediting period was established under v1.8 rules (5-year max, 2024-2029). The v2.0 10-year rule would apply to new projects or renewals.

## Core Credit Requirements
Credits must be:
- **Durable** — storage must last as defined by the applicable module
- **Additional** — would not have happened without carbon credit revenue
- **Scientifically quantified** — verifiable, reproducible methodology

## Document Structure
- **Protocols** — define specific CDR pathways (e.g., Bio Oil Geologic Storage)
- **Modules** — supplementary methodology components (e.g., storage, feedstock, transport)
- **PDD (Project Design Document)** — required for every project; basis for validation and ongoing evaluation

## Project Requirements
- Projects must submit a PDD covering: project description, baseline scenario, additionality demonstration, quantification methodology, monitoring plan, stakeholder engagement, risk assessment
- Crediting periods: max **5 years** (except Biosphere protocols); must re-validate if >12 months since last verification
- Projects must select and justify a baseline scenario

## Additionality Types
- **Financial** — project is not financially viable without credits
- **Common Practice** — activity is not standard practice
- **Environmental** — delivers environmental benefit beyond baseline
- **Regulatory** — not required by existing law/regulation

## Buffer Pools (Reversal Risk)
| Risk Level | Buffer Pool |
|------------|-------------|
| Very Low   | 2%          |
| Low        | 5%          |
| Medium     | 7%          |
| High       | 10-20%      |

Buffer credits are held in reserve; if a reversal occurs, buffer credits are cancelled to compensate.

## Uncertainty
- Uncertainty quantification is required for all key parameters
- Stepwise uncertainty assessment methodology

## Validation & Verification
- Performed by accredited VVBs (Validation & Verification Bodies)
- Validation: upfront review of PDD before project start
- Verification: ongoing review of monitoring reports to issue credits
- Projects must re-validate if there are material changes to methodology or if >12 months since last verification

## No Double Counting
- Credits cannot be issued for the same tonne twice
- Projects must demonstrate no overlap with other registries or compliance markets

## Reversals
- If stored carbon is released, buffer pool credits are cancelled
- Catastrophic reversals may require additional remediation
